Summary for Question 1

From our graph, it looks like, in terms of population density, Staten Island, while having the lowest number of cases overall, has the highest statistical murder flag per section, in comparison to the other boroughs. Conversely, the area with the highest number of cases, Brooklyn, had a much lower distribution of statistical murder cases in comparison to its over number of cases. All of the areas seem to have a statistical murder count about 1/3 of their total number of cases, which means the best for my model to choose is an area withe the lowest total number of cases, being Staten Island, followed by Manhattan, then Queens.

Summary of Question 3 These trends are very interesting because it really showcases human behaviors. During midyear, school is out, the weather is mostly favorable and travel is pretty high, resulting i more common foot traffic. During the weekend, there is usually more people free and available as well, which we can substantiate through our findings. The opposite can be said during February where it is usually the middle of winter, and going outside is less favorable. The same for our hours analysis, where the hours people are usually asleep and there is less foot traffic/ witnesses, gun violence tends to be higher.

In conclusion, the most favorable environment for gun violence to occur is during a time when people are generally free from school/ work (weekends), more mobile (mid-year) and have less witnesses to their crimes (late at night). If our model person wants to move to New York, knowing what conditions she should consider for her environment in order to be safe will better arm her with making the right decision for her.
